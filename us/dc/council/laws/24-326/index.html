D.C. Law 24-326. Advisory Neighborhood Commission Boundaries Act of 2022.

AN ACT

To establish boundaries for Advisory Neighborhood Commission areas and single-member districts within Advisory Neighborhood Commission areas, and to provide for each Advisory Neighborhood Commission to be the successor-in-interest to the assets, obligations, and legal interests of its predecessor.

BE IT ENACTED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, That this act may be cited as the "Advisory Neighborhood Commission Boundaries Act of 2022".

New Chapter 3A of Title 1

Sec. 2. Establishment.

Pursuant to section 738 of the District of Columbia Home Rule Act, approved December 24, 1973 (87 Stat. 824; D.C. Official Code § 1-207.38), and sections 3 and 4 of the Advisory Neighborhood Commissions Act of 1975, effective October 10, 1975 (D.C. Law 1-21; D.C. Official Code §§ 1-309.02, 1-309.03), there are hereby established the following Advisory Neighborhood Commission ("ANC") areas and single-member districts ("SMD") within ANC areas to be effective as of January 2, 2023, except as provided in subsection (b) of this section, the boundaries of which shall be depicted on the official maps of the District of Columbia according to the following legal descriptions:

Legislative History (LIMS)

Law 24-326, the “Advisory Neighborhood Commission Boundaries Act of 2022,” was introduced in the Council and assigned Bill No. 24-1124 which was retained by the Council. The bill was adopted on first and second readings on Dec. 6, 2022, and Dec. 20, 2022, respectively. After mayoral review, it was assigned Act No. 24-769 on Jan. 17, 2023, and transmitted to Congress for its review. D.C. Law 24-326 became effective Mar. 10, 2023.
